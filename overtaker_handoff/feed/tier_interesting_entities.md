# High-Value Tiering — Marquee Interesting Entities (verified shortlist)

_The genuine, review-worthy leads that survived 88-agent adversarial verification. Leads warranting confirmation — **not** findings of wrongdoing; no entity is accused._

## Headline findings

- The model's biggest dollar clusters are benign-by-structure, not red flags: the State's self-insured employee health-benefits program (BCBS-AZ, UnitedHealthcare at ADOA), AHCCCS Medicaid managed-care capitation, PSPRS/ASRS pension paydowns, Lottery prize-fund sweeps to Bank of America, ADOT highway-construction JVs, and university operating-lump-sum appropriations together account for most flagged value and resolve to routine entitlement, intergovernmental, pension, debt-service, or documented-contract flows.
- A single FY2021 data artifact drives ~70% of the new-vendor leads: vendor_id is NULL system-wide in FY2019-2020 and the vid numbering scheme was re-minted in FY2021, so long-standing payees (MedImpact, BCBS, ASU, CoreCivic, Centurion, five major ADOT contractors) are mislabeled 'new.' A payee-name resurfacing guard removes ~80% of FY2021 new-vendor fires while preserving the genuinely new ones.
- All 15 agency 'accountability-gap' clusters (~$5B+) are one systemic accounting convention, not missing accountability: ~99% are null/N-A payee on INTERNAL/JOURNAL-VOUCHER/COST-ALLOCATION rails — inter-fund transfers, debt service, benefits-trust legs, and Medicaid/pension postings that have no external payee by design. The triple_gap and placeholder_payee markers re-detect the internal-accounting rail and should be retired or gated to external rails only.
- After guardrails, a small high-quality shortlist of genuine leads survives — discretionary, concentrated, often sole-source spend to named private parties: Fondomonte ($7M federal-recovery payment to a Saudi-owned ag lessee), AshBritt ($194.7M border-barrier emergency contract), Active Resource Management ($12.5M water-credit purchase), an Arizona Holocaust-center earmark ($7M), Kinella Construction at the Legislature, and Weil Gotshal outside counsel. These are the items a bondholder should actually see.
- Prison-healthcare concentration carries a live external overlay: NaphCare's ~$1.4B statewide inmate-healthcare contract (and its predecessor Centurion) is structurally a single documented contract, but a Feb-2026 federal-court receivership over Arizona prison healthcare with power to renegotiate or terminate the contract makes it review-worthy beyond the data alone.
- Several markers fire on structure rather than behavior and need fixes: person_name_payee misfires on corporate names (PITNEY BOWES, RR DONNELLEY) and government offices (STATE TREASURER); agency_benford fails 100% of an agency's rows purely from the $25K high-value floor and payroll banding; offcontract is near-worthless where contract_number is 0% populated (all universities, ASRS, courts, ADOT capital); and nonstd_rail wrongly flags plain WARRANT, the Treasurer's standard external check rail.
- The duplicate-payment markers are ~95-97% line-split artifacts but retain a real residual: most 'duplicates' are one invoice decomposed into accounting lines sharing a single GAX/PRC document, but requiring genuinely distinct transaction_reference_ids isolates ~67 legitimate same-vendor, identical-amount, separate-document leads worth a double-payment look (e.g. Deloitte same-day warrant pairs).
- A persistent transparency texture, not wrongdoing: large dollars routinely flow to redacted, masked, or individual payees (NAME REDACTED, MISCELLANEOUS CUSTOMER, settlement-fund law firms and claimants) that the published checkbook does not name. This is best handled as a single agency-level disclosure-completeness note rather than thousands of per-item leads.
